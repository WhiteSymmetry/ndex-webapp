NDEx- the Network Data Exchange

NDEx will be a public website where scientists and organizations can share, store, manipulate, and publish biological network knowledge. It will be based on an open-source, web-based software system that will be coordinated with the Cytoscape project.

NDEx is a project of the Cytoscape Consortium in conjunction with the Ideker lab at the UCSD School of Medicine.

Technical details for NDEx are documented at the NDEx GitHub Repository Wiki.

The Need for a Network Commons

The scientific community needs a commons where the rapidly expanding corpus of biological network models created by researchers can be shared, discussed, reviewed, and used. This is especially true for network models produced via systematic mining and integration of large-scale molecular data.

Presently, many network models are available only as figures, tables, or supplementary information in articles, often published in journals that target bioinformatic audiences rather than those with biological, pharmaceutical, or clinical interests.

Although repositories of biological pathways and databases of molecular interactions are readily accessible, there is currently no public forum where biologists can easily exchange and publish computable network models of all types and formats.

Moreover, there is no central resource where network models of different types can be found by their relevance to particular genes, functions, or diseases, and there is a need for streamlined mechanisms whereby scientists can acquire the content of these networks in easily computable forms.

Finally, there is no facile infrastructure for pre-publication sharing and inspection of these complex models among collaborators, impeding biological interpretation and review, and slowing the development of biological network-related algorithms.

As a result, many network models remain largely inaccessible to those who have the most knowledge to interpret them and the most to gain from their successful interpretation.

NDEx and the Community

To address this need, the NDEx project will create a durable, collaborative web resource to capture knowledge of the structure and function of molecular networks. The NDEx system will be comprised of (1) a freely available, open-source server platform and (2) a public website built on that platform. Researchers will use NDEx to access, share, and publish biological knowledge in multiple network formats.

The NDEx project does not compete with existing pathway and interaction databases, such as Pathway Commons, KEGG, or Reactome; instead, NDEx provides a novel, common distribution channel for these efforts, preserving their identity and attribution rather than subsuming them.

The NDEx project is poised to play a role synergistic with existing programs and resources in systems biology and bioinformatics.

The Ideker lab is a member of the Sage Bionetworks Federation and, together with Sage, will fully integrate NDEx with the Synapse system.

The NDEx project will work with groups creating systems that provide access to clinical measurement data, pharmacological data, and other data relevant to research and drug discovery, such as eTRIKS/tranSMART and Open PHACTS, providing a storage and communication mechanism for networks derived by analyses of these data.

NDEx Users Store and Share Networks

As with popular file-sharing systems such as Google Docs, NDEx users will create accounts and then manage access to the content that they upload. In this example, Jane grants access permission to Cindy for a network that Jane owns.

Each user in the system has a home page, analogous to an account on LinkedIn or Facebook. At that page, the user can manage their account and other users can see the information and networks that the user has made public or has shared with them.

NDEx is a Unified Network Resource

NDEx will handle all common network data formats, including BioPAX, SBML, GPML, OpenBEL, PSI-MI, SIF, and XGMML. .

Scientists using NDEx will have a unified interface to search models of all types: simple and sophisticated, large and small, data-derived, text-mined, or manually curated. This juxtaposition of diverse networks expressed in a common environment will provide an unmatched opportunity to compare and contrast models, aiding researchers in hypothesis generation and model refinement.

Full semantic interoperability between existing network formats is an open issue, therefore the NDEx project will focus on task-oriented format translation services developed to support specific use cases.

NDEx Facilitates Collaboration

Groups in NDEx may correspond to organizations such as companies, academic institutions, or laboratories, or they may be informal associations created for purposes of collaboration.

In this example, Jane creates the E2F Working Group, then invites her collaborators to join. Together, they develop a pre-publication network that is private to the E2F working group.

NDEx is a Publication Medium

Beyond sharing, NDEx permits the publication of stable versions of networks. Networks can be explicitly created as dated versions including publication meta- data. Published versions are immutable and are assigned stable URIs and DOIs, making the networks and their contents suitable for reference by publications, by other networks, and by analytic applications.

In this example, the Jones Lab created a model of RBL2 and E2F4 interaction. When it was ready to publish, a locked - immutable - version was created and made publically accessible

The stability of published content is critical to the scientific process – in order to meaningfully review a published network or to build hypotheses or draw conclusions based on network knowledge, the referenced network must be a constant, immutable entity.

NDEx therefore distinguishes the act of publication from the granting of access: a network may be published but only accessible to a limited group.

Moreover, publication is also distinct from review – the NDEx site only plays the role of a “fair broker”, providing the infrastructure for networks to be stored, shared, and published. The NDEx site itself makes no claims about the value or correctness of any network content. Networks derive their credibility based on their publisher, or based on review by other scientists and organizations. The NDEx platform will thereby enable experimentation with novel forms of scientific review and discourse.

NDEx Accommodates Proprietary Networks

To facilitate the integration of public and private information, the NDEx server software can be deployed as a private site. Private networks can then be integrated with selected networks mirrored from the public NDEx site.

NDEx and Cytoscape

The NDEx project will be tightly linked to the core Cytoscape team and NDEx is planned as an integral part of future Cytoscape development.

As a first step in integration, a new Cytoscape App will enable users to access the NDEx website directly from Cytoscape, engaging the full range of Cytoscape tools to analyze and transform the networks stored on NDEx.

Users will be able to sign in to NDEx via the NDEx App, download networks to Cytoscape for processing and then store results in their account.

NDEx is a Hub for Applications

NDEx is based on a REST web API which will not only power the public website, but also enable access by other applications, including Cytoscape.

In the spirit of the successful history of Cytoscape plug-ins, the community will be supported in the creation of new utilities and analytic tools using NDEx via the API.

Custom applications and scripts will also be able to access and manipulate networks via the API, making it easy for scientists to develop novel network based analyses.
